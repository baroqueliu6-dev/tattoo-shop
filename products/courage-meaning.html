勇

📖 Historical Context

The character 勇 (yǒng) represents courage, bravery, and valor. In ancient Chinese philosophy, courage was considered one of the three universal virtues alongside wisdom (智) and benevolence (仁).

The character is composed of two parts: 甬 (a phonetic component suggesting movement) and 力 (strength/power), together implying the strength to move forward despite fear.

💪 Multiple Meanings

  • Bravery: Facing danger or difficulty without fear
  • Courage: The mental strength to overcome challenges
  • Valor: Heroic courage, especially in battle
  • Boldness: Willingness to take risks

🎯 Usage Examples

  • 勇敢 (Yǒnggǎn): Brave, courageous
  • 勇气 (Yǒngqì): Courage, guts
  • 勇士 (Yǒngshì): Warrior, brave person
  • 英勇 (Yīngyǒng): Heroic, valiant
  • 勇往直前 (Yǒngwǎngzhíqián): "March forward courageously" - an idiom meaning to advance bravely

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Similar Characters: Don't confuse with 通 (pass through) or 涌 (surge) which share the top component
  • Proportions: The top part should be slightly larger than the 力 radical
  • Placement: Works well on chest, arm, or back. The strong vertical structure suits vertical placement.

🎨 Tattoo Design Tips

  • Size: Minimum 2 inches for clarity
  • Style: Excellent for bold traditional, tribal, or brush stroke styles
  • Pairing: Can be combined with dragons, lions, or mountain imagery
  • Orientation: Both vertical and horizontal work well
