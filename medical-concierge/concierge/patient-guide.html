Patient Guide

Your Complete Guide to Medical Care in Beijing

Why this guide exists:

When you're ill, the hardest part isn't the language barrier or the distance. It's navigating an unfamiliar hospital, an unfamiliar system, and unfamiliar medical conditions while you're at your weakest — physically and mentally.

This guide walks you through every step, so you know what to expect. And when you need more help, we're here to guide you personally.

✈️ Before You Come

  • Medical Visa Requirements (S1/S2)

    Most international patients need a medical visa. We provide invitation letters and application guidance.

  • China Visa-Free Transit Policy

    Many international patients can enter Beijing without a visa for short medical consultations:

    • 72-Hour Visa-Free Transit: Available for citizens from 53 countries (US, UK, Canada, Australia, most EU countries). Valid for Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou.
    • 144-Hour Visa-Free Transit: Available for citizens from 53 countries. Valid for Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei region, Shanghai-Jiangsu-Zhejiang region, Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macau region.

    Requirements:

    • Valid passport (6+ months validity)
    • Confirmed onward ticket to third country (within 72/144 hours)
    • Valid visa for onward destination (if required)

    Note: For longer medical treatments, you'll need to apply for a medical visa (S1/S2). We can provide invitation letters.

    Check Your Eligibility: Contact us to verify if your nationality qualifies for visa-free transit.

  • Required Documents

    Passport, medical records, doctor's referral letter, financial proof, and visa application forms.

  • Travel Preparation Checklist

    Flight bookings, travel insurance, medication supply, power adapters, and emergency contacts.

  • Accommodation Near Hospitals

    We can recommend hotels and serviced apartments near your chosen hospital.
