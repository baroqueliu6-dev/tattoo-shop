🏥 Hospital Safety & Quality

Are Chinese hospitals safe for international patients?

Yes. Many of the premium private hospitals and elite public hospital international wings hold JCI accreditation.

This is a globally recognized gold standard indicating that a hospital meets the strictest protocols for:

  • Patient safety
  • Infection control
  • Continuous quality improvement

What is JCI accreditation?

Joint Commission International (JCI) accreditation is the global gold standard for healthcare quality and patient safety.

Hospitals with JCI accreditation must meet strict standards in:

  • Patient care and treatment
  • Infection prevention and control
  • Staff qualifications and training
  • Facility safety and security
  • Quality improvement programs

What are Grade 3A hospitals?

Grade 3A (三级甲等) is China's highest hospital rating, awarded to hospitals that demonstrate:

  • Advanced medical equipment and technology
  • Highly qualified medical staff
  • Comprehensive specialty departments
  • Research and teaching capabilities
  • Quality management systems

Only about 10% of Chinese hospitals achieve this top accreditation.

🛂 Visa & Travel

Do I need a visa for medical treatment in China?

For short medical visits (under 72-144 hours), citizens of 54 countries can enter China visa-free under the transit policy.

For longer treatments, you will need an S1 or S2 medical visa.

How do I get an S1/S2 medical visa?

To qualify for an S1/S2 medical visa, the Chinese consulate requires an official Invitation Letter from a Grade 3A hospital.

This letter must outline:

  • Your specific treatment plan
  • Estimated costs
  • Recovery duration

We handle the remote consultations necessary to secure this documentation on your behalf.

Which countries qualify for visa-free transit?

The 72/144-hour visa-free transit policy applies to citizens of 54 countries, including:

  • United States, Canada
  • United Kingdom, all EU member states
  • Australia, New Zealand
  • Japan, South Korea
  • And many more

💰 Payment & Insurance

Can I use my international health insurance at Chinese hospitals?

Chinese public hospitals use a localized billing system (CCD) rather than the international ICD-10 coding standard.

If your discharge summaries and official tax invoices (Fapiao) are not professionally translated and recoded, your international insurance claim will likely face automatic rejection.

Our Service: We manage this conversion process to secure your Guarantee of Payment (GOP).

Are foreign credit cards accepted for major surgeries in China?

Directly using foreign credit cards for large hospital bills is highly problematic.

Mobile wallets like WeChat Pay and Alipay severely restrict international cards:

  • Single transaction limit: 3,000 RMB (about $420)
  • Annual cap: About $2,000 total

We assist you in using compliant cross-border financial platforms to wire funds directly to the hospital's corporate accounts.
