💊 Medication Confusion: Don't Guess Your Dosage

📅 March 2026 | ⏱️ 5 min read
💊

Chinese prescriptions are written in Chinese characters. Dosage instructions are often unclear. This isn't just confusing — it's dangerous.

The Problem

When you get a prescription in China, you'll face:

  • ❌ Chinese-only labels: Drug names, dosages, instructions all in Mandarin
  • ❌ Unclear frequency: Is it 3 times daily or 3 pills total?
  • ❌ Unknown side effects: No English leaflet included
  • ❌ Drug interactions: Can't check if it conflicts with your regular meds
⚠️ Real Risk: 34% of expats report taking medication incorrectly due to language barriers (Beijing Expat Health Survey 2025)

Common Mistakes

❌ Dosage Confusion

"Take 1 pill, 3 times daily" becomes "Take 3 pills" → Triple overdose

❌ Timing Errors

"Before meals" vs "After meals" — affects drug absorption

❌ Duration Unclear

Stop too early (antibiotic resistance) or too late (side effects)

Real Example: What You Might See

Typical Chinese prescription label:

阿莫西林胶囊 0.25g × 24 粒

用法：口服，一次 2 粒，一日 3 次

(Translation: Amoxicillin 0.25g × 24 caps | Take 2 caps, 3 times daily, orally)
